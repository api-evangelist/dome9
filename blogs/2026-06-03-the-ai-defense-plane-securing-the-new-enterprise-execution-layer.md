This article argues that enterprises need a unified security architecture called the "AI Defense Plane" to manage AI risks across three operational areas: employee AI usage, AI-embedded applications, and autonomous agents. Traditional point controls cannot address AI security comprehensively because risks span multiple layers from initial prompts through data retrieval to agent actions. The authors call for integrated discovery, protection, governance, and assurance capabilities operating at runtime to secure the new enterprise execution layer.

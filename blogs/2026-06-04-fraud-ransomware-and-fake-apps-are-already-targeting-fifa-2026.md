Check Point Research has identified coordinated cyber threats targeting the FIFA World Cup 2026 across financial services, transportation, hospitality, and gambling sectors. Threat actors have already pre-positioned infrastructure including fraudulent cryptocurrency tokens, domain impersonations, fake apps, and ransomware capabilities timed to exploit the tournament's massive transaction volumes and global attention. Attackers are staging attacks in advance rather than opportunistically, with fake sportsbook apps and lookalike domains already registered and awaiting activation during peak betting and travel periods around the June 11 opening.
